# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Brazilian teachers at every level: AP, SAT, ENEM/vestibular, physics olympiads, math olympiads, everyday school subjects. They work in a hurry, often between classes or late at night, and need to produce polished materials without fighting software. They are subject experts, not designers or power users.

## Product Purpose

trasso lets teachers build printable exercise sheets and exam PDFs in minutes: type questions, pull from a reusable bank, import from a screenshot, or generate with AI. Output is a clean A4 PDF with a matching answer key. Users sign in to save sheets and questions and reuse them across classes and semesters.

## Brand Personality

Warm, encouraging, modern-academic. trasso speaks teacher-to-teacher: capable and clear, never a lecturer or a corporate tool. Friendly and confident. The UI feels like a well-designed notebook, not a SaaS dashboard or a government form.

## Anti-references

- **Old school edu-tech (Moodle, Google Classroom):** boxy, dense, utilitarian layouts, muted palettes, bureaucratic hierarchy, form-heavy UIs that feel like admin panels.
- **Exam software (Respondus, ProProfs, ExamSoft):** corporate test-admin aesthetic, dark tables, clinical spacing, impersonal tone, zero delight.
- Anything that makes a teacher feel like they are filing paperwork rather than creating teaching materials.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature.** Every extra click, modal, and confirmation is friction for someone between classes. Default to fast paths.
2. **Confidence without noise.** Clear visual hierarchy and ample space. No chrome for chrome's sake. The teacher's content is the hero, not the app's decoration.
3. **Academic without being archaic.** The violet-blue gradient, rounded geometry, and clean typography feel modern-academic: precise and approachable, not musty or bureaucratic.
4. **Delight at the margins.** Warmth and personality show up in small moments (greeting, empty states, status badges, micro-interactions), not in splashy decoration that competes with the work.
5. **Print first.** Every layout, typography decision, and color choice must look right on screen and on paper. A4 fidelity is non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Key needs: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large/bold text, full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings on all interactive elements, semantic HTML, screen reader-compatible form labels and error messages. Reduce-motion alternatives for all transitions. pt-BR throughout.
